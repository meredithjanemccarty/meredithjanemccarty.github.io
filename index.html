The primary focus of my research is two pronged:

1. How neural dynamics are integrated at different scales, be that population dynamics or the activity of single neurons, and how this information is captured by different tools.

I am currently a postdoctoral fellow in The Tandon Lab at the University of Texas Health Science Center in Houston, TX, USA, where I also completed my PhD research.

While I view science as a very creative process, I have invested much of my time into the visual arts, and share some of that work on this website. While I have dappled in many art forms — from video art to making paper collages and clay sculptures and the occasional food sculpture — I primarily enjoy photography.
